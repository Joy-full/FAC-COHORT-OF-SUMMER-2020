WhyMe

#Good Team Player

I lived in Malaysia for a few months as a member of volunteer team. I taught computer and programming skills to students there. In addition to daily class,my team had a lot of projects including creating mobile application and another promotional video. My team members were exhausted to meet tight deadlines in unfamiliar surroundings.

However, I often encouraged other members and picked up the slack for others so that my team members won’t give up on our tasks. At the end, we overcame it together and finally was awarded second prize in the organization. This made me a great supporter for a team.

I look forward to share my positive power and motivation at Founders and Coders.

#Contribution

Most importantly, I like not only to learn new things but also to share my knowledge. It’s really rewarding that people around me develop with my advice, and that process helps me a lot to growing up myself as well. That’s why I was a mentor for foreign students in my university and taught IT and English as a volunteer.

I can’t even imagine what Founders and Coders has provided. But I’d undoubtedly give back what I’ve got to community. It’s growth!

WhyFAC

#Peer-led Leaning

Every time I have challenged new things, I first started to work on myself. I’ve leveled up.

However, I’ve come to understand that something more is needed to reach even higher levels of improvement. That is other people with a common goal of improving. There would be virtually endless possibilities to what can be achieved.

I strongly believe that peer-led leaning is the most effective route into the tech industry. Also, I am impressive that graduates contribute back to the development of the program. They would understand well what learners want and need.

Synergy would be coming up!

#Principle of reciprocity

When I think of self-improvement, I think of how I could improve so that everyone around me gets to improve as well. For the sake of reciprocity, I hope to inspire the people around me to do the same well. This way, new highs of improvement can be reached. Each person should seek to improve themselves. And at the same time, they should seek to improve the relationship, so that the relationship itself can bear even more fruits.

Two heads are better than one!
